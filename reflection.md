# Reflection on Profile Comparisons

## Why Sunrise City Ranked First (High-Energy Pop)
Sunrise City ranked first because it matches two big categories at the same time (genre=pop and mood=happy), and it is also close to the requested energy level. In plain language: this song checks the most boxes at once, so it gets a strong total score.

## Pairwise Profile Comparisons

### High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi
High-Energy Pop pushes upbeat, lower-acoustic songs near energy 0.9, while Chill Lofi shifts toward calmer, more acoustic tracks near energy 0.35. This makes sense because these profiles ask for almost opposite vibes: party energy versus study/relax focus.

### High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock
Both profiles like high energy, so songs like Gym Hero can appear in both lists. The difference is that the Rock profile rewards intensity and rock genre, so Storm Runner rises to the top there, while Pop/Happy preferences keep Sunrise City near the top for the Pop profile.

### Chill Lofi vs Deep Intense Rock
Chill Lofi favors mellow and acoustic tracks, while Deep Intense Rock favors forceful and less-acoustic tracks. The output shift makes sense: one profile asks for quiet concentration, the other asks for aggressive workout-style energy.

## Plain-Language Note About Gym Hero
Gym Hero keeps showing up because it has very high energy and an intense mood, so it scores well for users who request energetic or intense music even when genre is not a perfect match. To a non-programmer: the system sees Gym Hero as a strong "high-energy default" track.
